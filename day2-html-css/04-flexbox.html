Lesson 4: Flexbox Layout

Flexbox is the modern way to arrange elements on a page. It lets you put things in rows, columns, center them, space them out -- all easily.

What is Flexbox?

Imagine you have a shelf and you're placing books on it.

  • The shelf is the PARENT container (the element with display: flex)
  • The books are the CHILDREN (the elements inside the container)

Flexbox lets you control:

  • Do the books go left-to-right or top-to-bottom?
  • Should they be bunched to the left? Centered? Spread out?
  • Should they be at the top, middle, or bottom of the shelf?

justify-content: Horizontal Alignment

justify-content controls how items are spread out along the main axis (horizontally, when items are in a row).

The HOLY GRAIL of centering: To perfectly center something both horizontally and vertically:
display: flex; justify-content: center; align-items: center;
That's it. 3 lines. Before Flexbox, this was incredibly hard!

The holy grail: justify-content: center + align-items: center

I'm perfectly centered!

When to use column:
Use flex-direction: column; when you want items stacked vertically but still want the alignment powers of Flexbox (centering, spacing, etc.).
This is common for mobile layouts, sidebars, and vertical menus.

Flexbox Quick Reference

Property Goes On What It Does
display: flexParentActivates Flexbox
flex-directionParentrow (horizontal) or column (vertical)
justify-contentParentAlign items along main axis
align-itemsParentAlign items along cross axis
gapParentSpace between items
flex-wrapParentAllow items to wrap to next line
flex: 1ChildMake item grow to fill available space
